Five-Course Meal

Amuse-Bouche

Watermelon & Feta Skewers with Mint

Recipe:

  1. Cut seedless watermelon and feta cheese into 1-inch cubes.
  2. Thread a watermelon cube, a feta cube, and a fresh mint leaf onto small skewers.
  3. Drizzle lightly with balsamic glaze (optional).
  4. Serve chilled.

Appetizer

Creamy Tomato Soup Shooters with Basil Oil

Recipe:

  1. Sauté diced onion and garlic in olive oil.
  2. Add crushed tomatoes, vegetable broth, and a pinch of sugar. Simmer until heated through.
  3. Blend the soup until smooth and creamy. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. For the basil oil, blend fresh basil leaves with olive oil until smooth. Strain if desired.
  5. Pour the warm soup into small glasses or shot glasses and drizzle with basil oil before serving.

Salad

Arugula Salad with Pear, Walnuts, and Goat Cheese Vinaigrette

Recipe:

  1. Wash and dry arugula. Slice a ripe pear. Toast walnut halves or pieces. Crumble goat cheese.
  2. For the vinaigrette, whisk together olive oil, red wine vinegar, Dijon mustard, honey (or maple syrup), salt, and pepper.
  3. In a bowl, gently toss the arugula with the pear slices, walnuts, and goat cheese.
  4. Drizzle the vinaigrette over the salad just before serving.

Main Course

Pan-Seared Salmon with Lemon-Dill Sauce and Roasted Asparagus

Recipe:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss asparagus spears with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast for 10-15 minutes until tender-crisp.
  2. Season salmon fillets with salt and pepper. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Sear the salmon skin-side down for 4-5 minutes until crispy. Flip and cook for another 3-4 minutes, or until cooked through.
  4. For the lemon-dill sauce, whisk together melted butter, lemon juice, fresh dill, and a pinch of salt.
  5. Serve the pan-seared salmon with the roasted asparagus and drizzle generously with the lemon-dill sauce.

Dessert

Berry Parfait with Yogurt and Granola

Recipe:

  1. Wash and prepare a mix of your favorite berries (strawberries sliced, blueberries and raspberries whole).
  2. Layer yogurt (Greek or regular), granola, and mixed berries in a glass or parfait dish.
  3. Repeat the layers.
  4. Garnish with a fresh mint leaf or a sprinkle of extra granola. Serve immediately or chill for later.
